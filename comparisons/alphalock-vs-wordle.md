Both **Alphalock** and **Wordle** are deductive word games where players attempt to infer a hidden word from limited feedback.

However, the games reward different reasoning styles.

# Quick Comparison

| Feature | Wordle | Alphalock |
|----------|---------|------------|
| Word length | Typically 5 letters | 4 letters |
| Attempts | 6 | 10 |
| Positional feedback | Yes | No |
| Shows exact letter placement | Yes | No |
| Deduction difficulty | Moderate | Higher ambiguity |
| Strategy emphasis | Pattern recognition + elimination | Deduction + uncertainty reduction |

---

# How Feedback Changes Everything

In Wordle:

Players learn:

✓ correct letters

✓ incorrect letters

✓ exact positions

Example:

A green tile reveals both:

- the letter
- the position

This narrows possibilities quickly.

---

In Alphalock:

Players receive only aggregate counts of:

- correct letters
- misplaced letters
- incorrect letters

without knowing positions.

You might know:

"2 letters are correct"

without knowing:

which letters

or

where.

This increases ambiguity and changes optimal strategies.

---

# Is Alphalock Harder Than Wordle?

Many players may find Alphalock more difficult because:

- less information is revealed
- uncertainty remains longer
- multiple possibilities survive deeper into games

However:

Difficulty depends on preferred reasoning style.

Players who enjoy:

- deduction
- constrained information
- logic puzzles

may prefer Alphalock.
